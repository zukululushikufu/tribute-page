Hirohiko Araki

Mangaka for hit series "JoJo's Bizarre Adventure"

"JoJo is an ode to life and a celebration of humanity. Without help from machines or technology, the main characters face danger with their bodies as their only weapon. For me it's a principle. Indeed I think that science doesn't necessarily make men happier."

Timeline of Araki's work

  • 1980 - Makes his debut with the one-shot manga Poker Under Arms at the Tezuka Award
  • 1981 - Outlaw Man
  • 1982 - Say Hi to Virginia
  • 1982 - B.T. The Wicked Boy
  • 1983 - Cool Shock B.T. was his first serialization, talking about a young magician who solves mysteries
  • 1984-1985 - His first manga series, Baoh was published on Weekly Shonen Jump, being the first series displaying his signature amount of over-the-top gore.
  • 1985-1986 Gorgeous Irene
  • 1986-2017 - JoJo's Bizarre Adventure was released. This is his magnum opus and has sold over 100 millions of copies all over the world being one of the most sold manga series of all times.
  • 1989 - Baoh was adapted into a single-episode OVA from Studio Pierrot
  • 1989-2003 - The Lives of Eccentrics
  • 1994 - Under Execution, Under Jailbreak
  • 1996 - Dolce, and His Master.
  • 1997-2015 - Thus Spoke Kishibe Rohan
  • 1999 - Deadman's Questions
  • 2002 - Oingo Boingo Brothers Adventure
  • 2010 - Rohan at the Louvre
